☕ Since Ancient Times

Ethiopian Coffee Ceremony

The heart of Ethiopian hospitality—a sacred ritual that builds community, fosters deep connection, and celebrates friendship.

Step 01

🌱 Roasting

Traditional Pan

Green Ethiopian coffee beans are washed and gently roasted over hot coals. Shaking the pan evenly fills the air with aromatic smoke—an ancient invite to step closer.

The roasting scent signals the official start of the ceremony.
Step 02

⚡ Grinding

Mukecha Tool

The roasted beans are ground using a heavy wooden mortar and pestle called a "mukecha". The rhythmic thumping sound echoes comfortably, signaling the freshness of the grind.

The ground texture is carefully checked to achieve optimal sensory release.
Step 03

🏺 Brewing

Clay Vessel

Ground grounds are poured into the "jebena"—a stunning clay pot featuring a spherical base and elegant neck. Placed on boiling coals, it slow-simmers into an intense extract.

Every single piece of traditional jebena is custom molded by local artisans.
Step 04

🎉 Serving

Three Traditions

The elixir is poured gracefully from an elevated height into small cups called "sini." Guests are invited to pass around snacks like popcorn, celebrating across three traditional stages.

The third and final round represents a critical communal token of blessing.

The Three Rounds of Blessings

Each pour possesses a unique strength, meaning, and rhythm

1

Abol

The First Brew

The strongest and deepest extract. Served immediately to capture full impact, setting a respectful atmosphere for coming dialogues.

2

Tona

The Second Brew

Lighter flavor balance. Hot water is added to original grounds to spark comfortable, extended social context and family stories.

3

Baraka

The Third Brew

The final blessing layer. "Baraka" directly translates to blessing—concluding with safe updates, peace keeping, and wishing well.

The Elements of Hospitality

Fresh Incense

Frankincense ("etan") burns continuously to clarify environmental clarity and welcome healthy paths.

Scattered Fresh Grass

The ground area features fresh natural grass carpets, symbolizing prosperity, freshness, and earthy connections.

Traditional Complementary Bites

Freshly popped corn kernels alongside crunchy roasted barley ("kolo") accent the robust bitter notes.
