☕ Since Ancient Times

Ethiopian Coffee Ceremony

The heart of Ethiopian hospitality — a sacred ritual that builds community and celebrates friendship.

Step 01

🌱 Roasting

Traditional Pan

Green Ethiopian coffee beans are washed and gently roasted over hot coals. Shaking the pan evenly fills the air with aromatic smoke — an ancient invite to step closer.

The roasting scent signals the official start of the ceremony.
Step 02

⚡ Grinding

Mukecha Tool

The roasted beans are ground using a wooden mortar and pestle called a "mukecha". The rhythmic thumping signals the freshness of the grind.

The ground texture is checked to achieve optimal sensory release.
Step 03

🏺 Brewing

Clay Vessel

Ground coffee is poured into the "jebena" — a clay pot with a spherical base and elegant neck. Placed on hot coals, it slow-simmers into an intense extract.

Every traditional jebena is custom molded by local artisans.
Step 04

🎉 Serving

Three Traditions

The coffee is poured gracefully from height into small cups called "sini." Guests enjoy snacks like popcorn across three traditional stages of the ceremony.

The third and final round represents a communal token of blessing.

The Three Rounds of Blessings

Each pour has a unique strength, meaning, and rhythm

1

Abol

The First Brew

The strongest extract. Served immediately to set a respectful atmosphere for coming dialogues.

2

Tona

The Second Brew

Lighter flavor. Hot water is added to spark comfortable social conversation and family stories.

3

Baraka

The Third Brew

"Baraka" means blessing — concluding with peace keeping and wishing well to all present.

The Elements of Hospitality

Fresh Incense

Frankincense ("etan") burns continuously to welcome guests and create a peaceful environment.

Scattered Fresh Grass

The ground features fresh natural grass carpets, symbolizing prosperity and earthy connections.

Traditional Complementary Bites

Freshly popped corn alongside roasted barley ("kolo") accent the robust coffee notes.
